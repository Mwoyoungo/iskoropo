Laundry Service
Active

The first service to build. A customer can book laundry pickup, washing, and delivery — while the provider manages everything from their phone.

👤 Customer Journey
Customer opens app and taps Laundry
When the customer opens the app, they see the home screen with service options. They tap "Laundry" and are taken straight into the laundry booking flow. Think of it like tapping a category on Uber Eats — one tap and you're in.
Steps to build
Home screen shows Laundry as a service category with an icon
Tapping Laundry navigates to the laundry intake screen
Intake screen has a friendly headline and a Start button
FlutterUI
Customer says how much laundry they have
Instead of typing or guessing, the customer sees three big picture cards on screen — Small (1–2 bags), Medium (3–4 bags), and Large (5+ bags). Each card shows the price range so they know what to expect before they even confirm. They just tap the one that matches their pile. No typing, no weighing, no confusion.
Steps to build
Three visual cards shown: Small / Medium / Large with illustrations
Each card shows price range in Rands (e.g. R60–R80)
Tapping a card highlights it and enables the Next button
Selected bag size saved so it can be used for pricing
FlutterUI
Customer selects any special items
Some clothes need extra care — delicates can't be washed on a normal cycle, white clothes need to be separated so they don't go grey, duvets take more space and time. The customer ticks any special items they have. Each one adds a small extra fee that shows up instantly on screen, so there are never any surprise charges at the end.
Steps to build
Special items displayed as tappable chips (can select multiple)
Items include: Delicates, Whites, Duvets, Baby clothes, Work uniforms
Price estimate updates live as items are selected or deselected
Selection saved so the provider sees exactly what to expect
FlutterUI
Customer picks a time + ironing upsell
The customer chooses when they want the laundry collected — Today, Tomorrow, or a specific date they pick from a calendar. Then the app shows one simple question: "Add ironing? +R30." This only appears for medium and large loads — ironing two t-shirts doesn't make sense, so we don't offer it for small loads. The final estimated price is shown clearly before they move on.
Steps to build
Time options: Today / Tomorrow / Pick a date (calendar picker)
Ironing toggle only shows for Medium and Large load sizes
Final price estimate displayed prominently before proceeding
Schedule and ironing preference saved for provider briefing
FlutterUI
AI summary — customer confirms before booking
Before any money moves, the customer sees a clear plain-English summary of everything they've selected. Something like: "3–4 bags of laundry, whites washed separately, ironing included, collected tomorrow — estimated R130 to R160." The AI takes their answers and writes this in a way that's easy to read. If something is wrong, they can go back and fix it. If everything looks good, they tap Confirm and get matched with a provider.
Steps to build
Summary card shows all selections in friendly plain words
Price range displayed prominently in large text
Edit button lets customer go back and change any selection
Gemini AI call generates the natural-language summary text
Confirm button moves to provider matching screen
AIFlutterFastify
Customer matched with a nearby provider
The app automatically finds the best available laundry provider nearby. It looks at distance, their star rating, whether they're free on the chosen day, and whether their price fits the customer's budget. The customer sees the provider's name, photo, rating, price, and how far they are. They can also see a second option if they're not happy with the first match.
Steps to build
Server finds providers matching service, location, date, and budget
Best match shown: name, rating, price, ETA in minutes
"See another option" button cycles through next best matches
Confirm Request button locks in the chosen provider
FastifyFirebaseFlutter
Customer pays to confirm the booking
The customer pays by card or instant bank transfer. Here's the important part — the money is held securely and NOT given to the provider yet. It's only released once the laundry is delivered back and the customer confirms everything is good. This protects the customer if something goes wrong, and ensures the provider gets paid once they complete the job properly.
Steps to build
Paystack payment screen (card + instant EFT options)
Payment held — not released until job is confirmed complete
Booking confirmation screen shown with reference number
SMS and push notification sent to confirm booking
FastifyFlutter
Customer tracks their laundry live
The customer can follow their laundry's journey on a map, just like tracking a delivery. The status updates automatically as the provider moves through each stage. A notification is sent at every stage change so the customer doesn't need to keep the app open — they'll know exactly what's happening without having to check.
Steps to build
Live map showing provider's dot moving toward customer
Status bar: Confirmed → Collected → Washing → Delivering → Done
Push notification sent automatically at each status change
In-app chat button to message the provider directly
FlutterFirebase
Customer rates the service
After the laundry is delivered, the customer gets a simple rating prompt. They give stars and can leave a comment if they want. This rating directly affects how often that provider appears in future matches — great providers get more work, poor ones get fewer bookings. It keeps quality high across the platform.
Steps to build
Rating screen appears after delivery is confirmed
Star rating (1–5) with optional written comment
Rating saved to Firebase and updates provider's score
Optional tip button for exceptional service
FlutterFirebase
🔧 Provider Journey
Provider sets up their laundry profile
Before a provider can receive any jobs, they go through a quick setup. They say they offer laundry, set their prices for small, medium, and large loads, add surcharges for special items, and draw their delivery area on a map. They also upload a photo of their ID — this is how Iskropo makes sure everyone on the platform is legitimate and trustworthy.
Steps to build
Provider selects Laundry as their service offering
Sets price per bag size (within Iskropo's allowed range)
Sets surcharges for special items (delicates, duvets, etc.)
Draws or selects their service area on a map
Uploads ID photo for Iskropo verification
FlutterFirebase
Provider gets notified of a new job
The moment a customer books nearby, the provider's phone makes a loud notification sound. They see a card with all the job details — how many bags, any special items, whether ironing is needed, the customer's address, and exactly how much they'll earn. They have 90 seconds to decide. If they accept, the job is theirs. If they decline or don't respond, it automatically goes to the next available provider.
Steps to build
Firebase push notification fires immediately when job is matched
Job card shows bag size, special items, ironing, address, earnings
90-second countdown timer visible on screen
Accept → active job screen; Decline → job offered to next provider
FastifyFirebaseFlutter
Provider manages job stages on their phone
Once a job is accepted, the provider's screen shows big clear buttons to tap as the job moves forward: On my way → I've collected the laundry → Currently washing → Out for delivery → Done. Each tap instantly updates what the customer sees on their phone. The customer always knows where their laundry is without having to call or message.
Steps to build
Status buttons: On my way / Collected / Washing / Delivering / Done
Each tap updates customer tracking screen in real time
Provider location shared live with customer during collection and delivery
Chat button lets provider message the customer from the job screen
FlutterFirebase
Provider gets paid after delivery
Once the customer confirms their laundry was delivered, the payment that was held since booking is automatically released. Iskropo's commission is deducted automatically — the provider can see exactly what was taken and why. The rest goes straight into their Iskropo wallet. They can request a bank payout at any time, and it arrives within one business day.
Steps to build
Payment auto-released when customer confirms delivery
Iskropo commission shown transparently (provider sees the breakdown)
Wallet balance updates immediately in provider app
Payout request sends money to provider's registered bank account
FastifyFirebaseFlutter
Provider identity verification
Every single provider must be verified by Iskropo before they can appear in any customer's search. They upload their ID and take a selfie. The Iskropo admin reviews both within 24 hours. If approved, the provider gets a blue verified badge on their profile that customers can see — this is a trust signal. Unverified providers are completely hidden from customers.
Steps to build
ID upload screen during provider onboarding
Selfie capture step for face verification
Admin dashboard shows all pending verifications in a queue
Approved providers get verified badge; rejected get a clear reason

Mobile Car Wash
Upcoming

A washer comes to wherever the customer's car is parked. Before and after photos protect both sides.

👤 Customer Journey
Customer picks car type and wash package
The customer selects their car size (hatchback, sedan, SUV, or bakkie) and the package they want — just the outside, a full wash, or a full wash plus vacuuming the inside. The price is set automatically. Then they drop a pin on the map showing exactly where their car is parked right now.
Steps to build
Car size selector: hatchback / sedan / SUV / bakkie
Package: Exterior only / Full wash / Full + interior vacuum
Price auto-calculated and shown clearly
Map pin drop: where is the car parked right now
FlutterUI
Before and after photo confirmation
When the washer arrives, they take a photo of the car before starting — so there's proof of what the car looked like before they touched it. When they're done, they take an after photo. The customer sees both photos side by side in the app before they confirm the job is done and release payment. This prevents any argument about whether the car was actually washed.
Steps to build
Customer receives before photo when washer arrives
Customer receives after photo when wash is complete
Side-by-side before/after view before confirming payment
FlutterFirebase
🔧 Provider Journey
Washer takes before + after photos
The washer's job screen prompts them to take a photo of the car when they arrive — this is mandatory, they can't mark themselves as started without it. When they're done washing, they take another photo and submit it. Only then does the customer get asked to confirm. This system means both sides are protected.
Steps to build
Before photo capture is mandatory to start the job
After photo required before marking job as done
Photos uploaded to Firebase Storage automatically
FlutterFirebase

Core Infrastructure — Powers Everything
Upcoming

These are the behind-the-scenes systems that every service depends on. They must be built alongside Phase 1.

🔐 Login and accounts
Every person using the app needs their own account — whether they're a customer booking laundry or a provider doing the washing. You can sign up and log in with your South African phone number (we send a one-time code via SMS) or with your Google account. Customers and providers have separate sign-up flows because they need different information.
Steps to build
Phone number OTP login via Firebase Auth (SMS code)
Google sign-in option
Separate onboarding flow for customer vs provider
Profile screen showing booking history and settings
Firebase AuthFlutter
📍 Live location tracking
When a provider is on their way to a customer, the customer sees a live dot on the map showing exactly where the provider is and moving in real time — just like Uber. The provider's phone sends their GPS location every few seconds while a job is active. As soon as the job is marked done, location sharing stops automatically to protect the provider's privacy.
Steps to build
Provider app sends GPS location every 5 seconds during active job
Customer map updates live via Firebase Realtime Database
Location sharing stops automatically when job is Done
Battery-efficient background GPS on provider's phone
FirebaseFlutter
🔔 Push notifications
The app sends automatic alerts at key moments so nobody misses anything important. Customers get notified when their booking is confirmed, when their provider is on the way, and when the job is done. Providers get a loud sound alert the moment a new job is available near them — speed matters here because they only have 90 seconds to respond.
Steps to build
Firebase Cloud Messaging set up for both iOS and Android
Customer alerts: booking confirmed / provider on way / job done
Provider alert: new job nearby (loud sound, immediate)
Tapping a notification opens the relevant screen in the app
Firebase FCMFlutter
💳 Payments via Paystack
Customers pay by card or instant bank transfer (EFT). When they book, the money is held securely — think of it like Takealot holding your payment until the item arrives. The money is only sent to the provider once the job is confirmed done. Iskropo's cut is automatically taken before the provider receives anything. If a job is cancelled before it starts, the customer gets a full refund.
Steps to build
Paystack SDK integrated into the Flutter app
Card and instant EFT payment options available
Payment held on booking, released on job completion
Iskropo commission deducted automatically on the Fastify server
Refund flow for cancellations before job starts
FastifyFlutter
💬 In-app chat between customer and provider
Once a job is booked, the customer and provider can message each other directly inside the app — no need to share personal phone numbers. This is useful for practical things: "I'm outside the gate," "please use the back entrance," or "can you add fabric softener." The chat is only active while a job is in progress. After the job is done, the chat closes.
Steps to build
Chat screen accessible from the active job tracking screen
Real-time messages via Firebase Realtime Database
Chat locked automatically after job is marked Done
Report message button for basic moderation
FirebaseFlutter
🖥️ Admin dashboard for Iskropo team
Behind the scenes, the Iskropo team has a private web page (not part of the mobile app) where they can manage everything. They can see all users and providers, approve new providers who've submitted their ID, watch all active bookings happening right now, see how much money has moved through the platform, and step in to resolve any disputes between a customer and a provider.
Steps to build
User list: view all customers and providers
Provider verification queue: approve or reject with reason
Active and past bookings overview
Revenue and commission summary dashboard
Dispute management: view, comment, and resolve complaints

⚙️ Tech Stack — What Powers Iskropo
📱
Flutter
One codebase that builds both the iPhone and Android apps simultaneously. Faster to build, easier to maintain.
🔥
Firebase
Google's platform that handles user accounts, stores all data, enables live location tracking, and sends push notifications.
⚡
Fastify Server
The brain behind the scenes. Handles bookings, payment logic, provider matching, and AI requests. Built for speed.
🧠
Gemini AI
Google's AI that understands what customers are asking for, generates plain-English summaries, and powers smart suggestions.
💳
Paystack
South African payment gateway. Accepts cards and instant EFT. Holds money until jobs are completed, then pays providers.
🗺️
Google Maps
Shows live provider locations on the map, calculates distances, and lets customers and providers see each other's position.
